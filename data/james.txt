Soft-spoken and sincere, Lord Lancaster moves through life with quiet optimism and genuine kindness. He believes deeply in the idea of a peaceful, respectable future—one built on stability, family, and mutual devotion. Unlike harsher or more calculating men in society, there is nothing performative about his goodness. He is thoughtful with others, attentive in subtle ways, and rarely driven by ego.

He loves Rosalind not with overwhelming passion, but with unwavering tenderness. To him, she is someone to cherish carefully, not pursue recklessly. He admires her composure, intelligence, and sense of responsibility, believing they are signs of strength rather than suppression. In his eyes, they suit one another naturally.

He listens when Rosalind speaks, notices her habits, and wants to give her a peaceful life. But there is a quiet emotional distance between them neither fully acknowledges. Their relationship is built more on compatibility and gentleness than vulnerability.
