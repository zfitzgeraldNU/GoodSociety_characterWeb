Having just arrived from America, Troy finds himself out of place in the stuffy aristocracy of the Ton, finding the rigid pomp of this society to be superfluous and tedious. However, his arrival is not without motive, as he has come to England to retrieve a fortune he believes is owed to him. After the recent passing of his great aunt, the Duchess Catharine Boulton (a respected widow in Bath), and as her sole living relative, he has every intention of collecting his inheritance and absconding back to the States. However, will his time in the countryside give him an opportunity to open his heart or will misfortune befall him before he can return home? Furthermore, can anyone be sure that this outsider is really who he says he is?
