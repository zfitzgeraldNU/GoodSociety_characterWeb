Elenor Huntley, born Elenor Fletcher, returns to England beneath the quiet dignity of widowhood, her name still tied to the first husband she lost. Once a hopeful bride disillusioned by the realities of marriage, she has since cultivated a careful composure, moving through the ton with measured charm and an instinct for survival. Having spent time away from familiar circles, her past remains pleasantly indistinct, her circumstances comfortably secured. With old connections to manage and new prospects to consider, her reappearance marks the beginning of a deliberate course — the pursuit of another advantageous marriage.
